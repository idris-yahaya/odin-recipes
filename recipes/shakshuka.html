Shakshuka

Description

Shakshuka is a flavorful and hearty dish originating from North Africa, particularly Tunisia, and popular throughout the Middle East. It consists of poached eggs in a rich, spicy tomato and pepper sauce, typically seasoned with aromatic spices like cumin, paprika, and sometimes harissa paste. Shakshuka is often served with crusty bread or pita, making it a satisfying meal any time of day.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4-6 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional, for serving)
  • Crusty bread or pita, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or cast-iron pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and diced bell peppers, cooking until the peppers are softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the ground cumin, paprika, coriander, and cayenne pepper to the skillet, stirring well to coat the vegetables with the spices.
  5. Pour in the diced tomatoes and tomato paste, stirring to combine everything thoroughly. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 10-15 minutes, until it thickens slightly.
  6. Using a spoon, create small wells in the sauce for the eggs.
  7. Crack the eggs, one at a time, into the wells you created in the sauce.
  8. Season the eggs with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Cover the skillet and let the shakshuka cook for about 5-8 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny.
  10. Once the eggs are cooked to your liking, remove the skillet from heat.
  11. Garnish the shakshuka with chopped cilantro or parsley and crumbled feta cheese if desired.
  12. Serve the shakshuka hot, straight from the skillet, with crusty bread or pita for dipping and scooping up the delicious sauce. Enjoy!
